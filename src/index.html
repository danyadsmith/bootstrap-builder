Krug’s First Law of Usability

Don’t make me think!

For as long as I can remember, I’ve been telling people that this is my first law of usability.

It’s the overriding principle—the ultimate tie breaker when deciding whether a design works or it doesn’t. If you have room in your head for only one usability rule, make it this one.

For instance, it means that as far as humanly possible, when I look at a Web page it should be self-evident. Obvious. Self-explanatory.

Steve Krug, Don’t Make Me Think, Revisited
